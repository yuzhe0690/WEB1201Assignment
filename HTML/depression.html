Depression

What Is Depression ?


Depression is highly different from normal mood swings in everyday life especially reoccurrence with severe conditions. A person that experienced depression would lose pleasure, interest, or both in any activity most of the day. It is also likely for other symptoms to appear at the same time such as hopelessness, suicidal thoughts, poor concentration, extensive guilt, low self-worth, troubled sleeping, constant sleepiness, and change in appetite & weight (World Health Organization, 2021).


When feeling depressed, the duration where it lasts for each individual varies where some would only experience it for 2 weeks while some experience longer durations. The severity of symptoms, number of symptoms, and impact on an individual’s area of functioning can be used to determine the severity of the depression (WHO, 2021).


Treatments & Prevention


  • Different severity of depression has different kinds of treatments such as therapy and medication (antidepressants). Antidepressants will only be used for patients that are old enough and don’t have mild depression. Antidepressants will not be used for children and they will not be introduced to adolescents as first-line treatment (WHO, 2021).
  • Some actions that a patient could take immediately when having depression are avoiding alcohol, finding ways to deal with stress, spending time with people around them, and more (Sacks. E., 2021).
  • Some preventions that could also be taken for depression are reaching out to others when needing help, improving self esteem, handling stress, getting enough sleep, eating well, exercising, and more (Sacks. E., 2021).
